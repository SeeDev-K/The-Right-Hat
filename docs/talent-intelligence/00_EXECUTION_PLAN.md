# The Right Hat — Talent Intelligence Strategy Execution Plan

Date: 2026-07-10
Status: Active workstream

## Strategic principle

The Right Hat must not launch as a generic combination of LinkedIn, Upwork, Indeed, GitHub and Wellfound. That framing is a long-term platform vision, not a credible launch product.

The execution strategy is therefore designed around a narrow, high-trust market entry:

> The Right Hat becomes an AI-powered verified talent network for cybersecurity, AI, cloud and digital engineering, initially serving Morocco and francophone Africa, then expanding toward European and international employers.

The first defensible asset is not a job board. It is a trusted evidence layer that proves what a professional can actually do.

## Phase 1 — Strategic Truth

Purpose: determine whether the opportunity deserves investment and define the market entry.

Deliverables:
- problem statement;
- ideal customer profile;
- initial talent segment;
- geographic entry market;
- founder-market fit;
- competitive wedge;
- cold-start strategy;
- validation plan;
- Go / Modify / Stop verdict.

Decision gate:
- at least 5 employer design partners;
- at least 3 written or recorded willingness-to-pay signals;
- at least 100 qualified talents interested in verification;
- one repeatable hiring pain with a measurable business cost.

## Phase 2 — Market and Competition

Purpose: quantify the opportunity and understand the real competitive battlefield.

Deliverables:
- TAM, SAM and bottom-up SOM;
- Morocco, francophone Africa and Europe market segmentation;
- competitor matrix covering local, regional and global players;
- alternative workflows used by employers today;
- positioning map;
- white-space opportunities;
- regulatory landscape for AI hiring.

Decision gate:
- market entry segment large enough to support a venture-scale expansion path;
- clear differentiation from ReKrute, Jobzyn, LinkedIn, Andela, Upwork and traditional agencies;
- no dependency on a feature that incumbents can copy without the underlying data asset.

## Phase 3 — Product Definition and MVP

Purpose: define the smallest product that creates paid value.

Initial product hypothesis:
1. Verified Talent Passport;
2. structured skills and evidence graph;
3. role brief and semantic talent search;
4. explainable AI shortlist;
5. recruiter workspace;
6. interview and evaluation workflow;
7. human-reviewed verification;
8. candidate consent and visibility controls.

Explicitly deferred:
- general social feed;
- full freelance escrow;
- payroll and employer-of-record;
- advertising marketplace;
- mass learning catalogue;
- generic all-industry recruitment;
- automated rejection without human review;
- universal reputation score.

Decision gate:
- employers can reach a qualified shortlist materially faster;
- candidates perceive verification as valuable rather than punitive;
- at least 3 paid pilots completed;
- shortlist-to-interview conversion reaches an acceptable baseline.

## Phase 4 — AI, Data and Trust Strategy

Purpose: identify where AI creates genuine value and where it introduces unacceptable risk.

Deliverables:
- talent graph data model;
- evidence taxonomy;
- matching architecture;
- recruiter copilot scope;
- candidate career assistant scope;
- evaluation datasets;
- explainability model;
- human-in-the-loop checkpoints;
- bias and fairness testing;
- model cost controls;
- privacy and consent model.

Hard rules:
- AI may recommend, summarize and prioritize;
- AI may not make an irreversible hiring rejection alone;
- sensitive traits must not be inferred;
- personality and culture-fit scoring must not become opaque exclusion mechanisms;
- every high-impact recommendation must expose evidence and confidence.

## Phase 5 — Business Model and Go-to-Market

Purpose: find the fastest path to revenue and repeatable distribution.

Initial revenue ladder:
1. concierge sourcing and verified shortlist fee;
2. success fee for completed hires;
3. recruiter workspace subscription;
4. enterprise talent intelligence subscription;
5. skills verification and credential services;
6. aggregated and anonymized market intelligence;
7. API access for ATS, universities and employers.

Go-to-market channels:
- founder-led sales;
- cybersecurity, cloud and AI communities;
- universities and bootcamps;
- employer design partnerships;
- sector associations;
- public and private digital-skills initiatives;
- diaspora and nearshore hiring networks.

Decision gate:
- repeatable acquisition channel;
- repeat employer usage;
- positive contribution margin on assisted hiring;
- evidence that software can replace part of the concierge work.

## Phase 6 — Technical Architecture and Security

Purpose: design the platform for multi-tenancy, privacy, explainability and growth.

Deliverables:
- frontend and backend architecture;
- multi-tenant data isolation;
- RBAC and ABAC;
- search and vector infrastructure;
- verification workflow;
- audit trails;
- consent and retention engine;
- secure document storage;
- API gateway;
- observability;
- CI/CD security;
- disaster recovery;
- GDPR and Moroccan data-protection mapping.

Decision gate:
- no cross-tenant data exposure;
- auditable hiring recommendations;
- export and deletion rights supported;
- data collection limited to product necessity;
- production security review completed.

## Phase 7 — Red Team the Business

Purpose: actively attempt to invalidate the strategy.

Questions:
- Why will companies refuse to pay?
- Why will strong candidates refuse verification?
- Why can LinkedIn, ReKrute or Andela copy the offer?
- Why will the marketplace remain empty?
- Why will AI matching fail in real hiring?
- Why will employers continue using WhatsApp, referrals and agencies?
- Why will compliance costs slow expansion?
- Why will the data never become a moat?

Decision gate:
- top 10 existential risks have owners and mitigations;
- kill criteria are defined;
- the product survives at least one adversarial review by external recruiters and candidates.

## Phase 8 — Founder Decision Memo

Purpose: turn research into an executable company plan.

Final deliverables:
- final vision and positioning;
- one primary ICP;
- one launch geography;
- one core workflow;
- twelve-month roadmap;
- staffing plan;
- budget and funding requirement;
- KPI tree;
- pricing strategy;
- fundraising narrative;
- 30-day execution plan;
- explicit list of features and markets not pursued.

## North Star hypothesis

> Number of successful verified talent engagements completed through TRH, where both employer and talent confirm the match met expectations.

Supporting metrics:
- time to qualified shortlist;
- shortlist-to-interview rate;
- interview-to-hire rate;
- employer repeat rate;
- verified profile completion rate;
- candidate response rate;
- candidate and employer trust score;
- percentage of recommendations with accepted evidence;
- gross margin per successful engagement.

## Strategic boundaries

TRH will not:
- sell identifiable candidate data;
- create a universal human score;
- automate final hiring decisions;
- launch every marketplace category simultaneously;
- become a generic social network before solving a paid hiring problem;
- treat AI-generated claims as verified evidence;
- expand internationally before proving one repeatable market entry.
